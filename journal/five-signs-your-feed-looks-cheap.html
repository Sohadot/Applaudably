Five signs your Instagram feed looks cheap (and the fixes)

None of these mistakes mean your product is bad. But each one quietly tells visitors "amateur" — and each one can be fixed in minutes once you see it.

1. Too many fonts

Four typefaces across nine posts reads as chaos. Premium brands use one display face and one supporting face, everywhere, forever. Fix: pick a pairing and delete every other font from your templates.

2. A palette that drifts

If your pinks are slightly different in every post, the grid never settles into a brand. Fix: choose five exact hex codes and use only those. Write them down; guessing is how drift happens.

3. Crowded layouts

Text pushed to the edges, three messages per post, no breathing room. Luxury is mostly empty space. Fix: one message per post, generous margins, and let the caption carry the detail.

4. Inconsistent margins and alignment

When text sits at a different height in every post, the grid looks assembled by different people. Fix: use templates with fixed text zones so alignment repeats automatically.

5. Heavy effects

Drop shadows, glows, thick outlines and busy stickers date a feed instantly. Fix: delete the effects. Flat color, clean type and a good photo will always look more expensive.

The shortcut

Every fix above is really one fix: work inside a disciplined template system instead of designing each post from scratch. That's exactly what our kits are — the discipline, pre-built.
