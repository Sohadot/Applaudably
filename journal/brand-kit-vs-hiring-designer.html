Brand kit or brand designer? An honest comparison

We sell brand kits, so you might expect this article to end with "never hire a designer." It won't. Custom brand design is genuinely valuable — at the right moment. The mistake most new founders make is buying it at the wrong one.

What a designer actually gives you

A good identity designer gives you strategy, originality and ownership: a mark built around your specific story, tested against your specific competitors, delivered with files and guidelines. For an established brand with revenue and a clear positioning, that's worth every dollar of a $3,000–$8,000 project.

Why it's often wasted on a brand-new brand

New brands change. Your first year will reshape your audience, your bestsellers, maybe your name. Commissioning a custom identity before that settling happens means paying premium prices to brand a moving target — and many founders end up rebranding within eighteen months anyway.

What a premium kit gives you instead

A well-made brand kit encodes the same craft decisions — palette discipline, type pairing, editorial layout — into templates you can apply today and evolve tomorrow. You get roughly 80% of the visual result at around 2% of the cost, with zero waiting. What you give up is exclusivity and bespoke strategy, which matter far less at launch than momentum does.

Our honest recommendation

Start with a kit. Launch, sell, learn who your customer actually is. When the brand has proven itself and the visuals start feeling too small for it — that's the moment a custom designer becomes a great investment, and you'll brief them ten times better for having lived with a real identity first.
